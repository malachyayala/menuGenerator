**Monday**

*   **Recipe Name:** Spicy Chili Crisp Noodles with Ground Pork
*   **Ingredients:**
    *   Udon Noodles: 2 Packs
    *   Ground Pork: 1 lb
    *   Spicy Chili Crisp: 1 tablespoon
    *   Soy Sauce: (Missing. Add to Shopping List; use Worcestershire sauce as sub)
    *   Chopped Garlic: 1 teaspoon
    *   Sesame Seeds: 1 teaspoon
    *   Green Onions: (Missing. Add to Shopping List)
*   **Detailed Instructions:**
    1.  Cook udon noodles according to package directions. Drain and set aside.
    2.  Brown ground pork in a pan over medium heat. Drain excess fat.
    3.  Add soy sauce (or Worcestershire), chopped garlic, and spicy chili crisp to the pork. Stir to combine.
    4.  Add the noodles to the pan and toss to coat.
    5.  Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions.
*   **Estimated Prep Time:** 5 minutes
*   **Estimated Cook Time:** 15 minutes
*   **Original Recipe Link:** Adapted from various recipes, e.g., [https://www.justonecookbook.com/spicy-sesame-noodles/](https://www.justonecookbook.com/spicy-sesame-noodles/)
*   **Leftover Potential:** Good for lunch the next day.

*   **Ingredients:**
    *   Rotini: 8 oz
    *   Tomato Paste: 3 oz (all)
    *   Garlic Powder: 1/2 teaspoon
    *   Onion Powder: (Missing. Add to Shopping List)
    *   Dried Oregano: (Missing. Add to Shopping List)
    *   Red Pepper Flakes: (Missing. Add to Shopping List, can use cayenne pepper as a sub)
    *   Olive Oil: (Missing. Add to Shopping List)
    *   Salt: to taste
    *   Parmesan Cheese: (Missing. Add to Shopping List)
*   **Detailed Instructions:**
    1.  Cook rotini according to package directions. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.
    2.  While pasta is cooking, heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add tomato paste and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until it darkens slightly.
    3.  Stir in garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper.
    4.  Add the cooked pasta to the sauce and toss to coat. Add pasta water as needed to create a creamy sauce.
    5.  Serve with grated Parmesan cheese.
*   **Estimated Prep Time:** 5 minutes
*   **Estimated Cook Time:** 20 minutes
*   **Original Recipe Link:** Adapted from various recipes.
*   **Leftover Potential:** Good for lunch the next day.
